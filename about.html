✦ About the shelf

MythShelf is where reading becomes mythic.

MythShelf is a personal archive of fantasy, science fiction, horror, classics, local lore, unfinished portals and books that shaped my imagination. It is part reading journal, part recommendation system, and part doorway into the kinds of stories I want to write.

What this is

A shelf with a memory.

This is not meant to be a formal book review site. It is a personal map of books I have read, paused, remembered, returned to, or still plan to explore.

Reading archive

The Full Shelf keeps track of books by series, subgenre, reading era, status, format and mood.

Recommendation tools

Shelf Recommends lets the archive suggest books through archetypes, themes, series order and book-to-book paths.

Story fuel

The shelf also points toward fantasy-horror interests: myth, monsters, power, memory, rebellion, dread and transformation.

Why MythShelf exists

From reading history to story compass.

The point is not just to list books. The point is to understand what kinds of stories keep returning: the rebel, the shadow, the trickster, the magician, the crown, the monster, the haunted place, the impossible transformation.

The bridge to original writing

I am interested in fantasy-horror, mythic systems, Caribbean memory, dark transformation, old gods, monsters, empire, rebellion and stories where the supernatural reveals something deeper about people and place. MythShelf is one way of tracing the books and patterns that feed that imagination.
